| Overview of the Amendments and Revisions to the General Requirements Adopted by the Volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition | The Volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia mainly contains general requirements, guidelines, and standards for pharmaceutical excipients. The general requirements include three parts: general requirements for dosage forms, general requirements for other pharmaceutical substances, and general testing methods. These are the essential requirements for drug standards and the foundation of the pharmacopoeia standards. In the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2015 Edition, for the first time, the general requirements for dosage forms, general testing methods, and guidelines were integrated and listed separately as the general requirements section in the Volume IV, achieving the coordination and unification of the common technical requirements and testing methods in all volumes of the pharmacopoeia. Through a decade of practice, especially with the implementation of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition and its supplementary volumes, and in combination with the promotion of the transformation and implementation of the guidelines of the International Council for Harmonization of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for Human Use (ICH) in China, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition has kept track of the frontiers of international pharmaceutical regulatory scientific research, aligned with international standards, scientifically and systematically amended or revised the general requirements in light of the dynamics of the pharmaceutical industry all over the China in the new era . Based on the requirements of risk assessment and quality control of the life cycle of drugs, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition (Volume IV) has optimized and improved the working mechanism for formulating and revising national drug standards with the Chinese Pharmacopoeia as the core. This has laid a solid foundation for establishing the most stringent drug standards, enhancing the safety and effectiveness of medical products, and promoting the high-quality development of the pharmaceutical industry. This article comprehensively introduces the elaboration process, main characteristics, and the contents of amendments and revisions of the general requirements and guidelines of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition.<br>1 Guiding Ideology and Compilation Process<br>1.1 Guiding Ideology<br>Guided by Xi Jinping Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, we have fully implemented the spirits of the 20th National Congress of the Communist Party of China and all plenary sessions of the 20th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China. We have earnestly implemented General Secretary Xi Jinping's requirement of the four strictest requirements. Adhering to the people-centered concept, we have practiced the new development concepts of innovation, coordination, green development, openness, and sharing. In accordance with the requirements of the Measures for the Administration of Drug Standards on the formulation and revision of national drug standards, we have adhered to the principles of being scientific and standardized, advanced and practical, as well as open and transparent. Taking the improvement of the drug standard working mechanism as the starting point, we have ensured safety and adhered to the bottom line, and promoted development and pursued high standards. According to the overall thinking and work objectives of the compilation outline, drawing on international standards and based on the research projects and research data on the formulation and revision of drug standards, we have continuously improved the construction of the general requirements system of Volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia. The formulation and revision work has become more rigorous and reasonable, and is more coordinated with international standards. The following key tasks have been mainly carried out: In the part of General Requirements for Dosage Forms, aiming at the development needs of the industry and key common technologies, focusing on complex drug delivery systems such as semi-solid preparations, inhalation and nasal sprays, the critical quality attributes indicators of preparations have been amended and revised. Attention has been paid to the coordination and collaboration between the General Requirements for Dosage Forms and the general physical and chemical provisions to ensure stability of the quality of preparations and batch to batch consistency. In the part of general testing methods and guidelines, the coordination with international standards such as ICH guideline has been strengthened, and the application of advanced and mature testing technologies has been continuously expanded. The microbial control system and the molecular biology testing technology system based on risk assessment have been improved. The design, establishment and verification of bioactivity assays have been further standardized, and the drug quality control throughout the whole process and the full life cycle has been strengthened. The concept of green environmental protection has been practiced, and alternative methods for toxic and harmful reagents have been formulated and revised. The working mechanism for formulating and revising national standards for traditional Chinese medicine has been optimized and improved, technical reserves have been strengthened, the level of quality control of traditional Chinese medicine has been enhanced, and its leading role has been brought into play to promote the revitalization and development of traditional Chinese medicine. Provide useful and effective regulatory scientific tools, standards and methods for the quality control of the full life cycle of drugs.<br>1.2 Compilation Process<br>In September 2022, shortly after the establishment of the 12th Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission, in accordance with the principles of overall coordination, advance planning, and comprehensive deployment, and with the compilation objective of ensuring the safety, effectiveness, and controllable quality of drugs, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission meticulously organized, carefully arranged, and carried out solid work. It formulated the compilation outlines and work plans for relevant specialized committees of Volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition. From October 2022 to March 2023, plenary meetings of the specialized committees of the 12th session, including those for physical and chemical analysis, preparations, microbiology, biological assay, pharmaceutical excipients, pharmaceutical packaging materials, and reference substances, were respectively held in the form of video conferences. All members of the corresponding specialized committees of the 12th Pharmacopoeia Commission, leaders of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission, and business personnel from relevant departments participated in the meetings respectively. The meetings provided training on how commissioners should perform their duties. They introduced to the participating commissioners and experts the legal status of the pharmacopoeia, its historical inheritance, as well as the responsibilities, work requirements and disciplines of commissioners, and especially explained the relevant requirements of the Constitution of the Pharmacopoeia Commission and the Measures for the Management of Commissioners. The meetings also put forward suggestions for the work of each specialized committee for the committees to study.<br>In order to implement the deployment of the Planning for National Drug Safety and Promotion of High-quality Development for the 14th Five-Year Plan Period and various compilation tasks of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, each specialized committee, after research, has in principle adopted the work plan of this session of the specialized committee. According to the work plan, each specialized committee organized and carried out a series of research work on general requirement topics. A total of 327 projects for improving national drug standards were established (72 projects in 2020, 50 projects in 2021, 54 projects in 2022, 98 projects in 2023, and 53 projects in 2024). Numerous domestic universities, scientific research institutions, drug inspection agencies, societies and associations and enterprises have actively participated in the projects for formulating and revising national pharmacopoeia standards, and have achieved a number of high-quality scientific research achievements, laying a solid scientific foundation for improving the level of general requirements of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition.<br>Guided by the most rigorous standards, the relevant specialized committees of this session have strictly reviewed and adhered to the pharmacopoeia admission standards. They have organized and held more than 60 specialized committee meetings, 3 enterprise communication meetings, and 5 project investigation activities. Nearly 200 draft standards were deliberated, and more than 3,000 pieces of feedback were reviewed. During the compilation process of the general requirements of Volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, on the basis of ensuring applicability, the guideline of the International Council for Harmonization of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for Human Use (ICH) were actively transformed and implemented, especially the ICH guideline Q4B pharmacopoeias. Except for the general chapter and guideline of biological products and pharmaceutical packaging materials, a total of 56 items have been newly added and 126 items have been revised in the general requirements of Volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition. During the process of formulating and revising the general requirements of this edition of Pharmacopoeia, emphasis was placed on strengthening coordination with drug regulatory, evaluation, and inspection institutions. The 9011 Guideline for in vivo Bioavailability and Bioequivalence Studies of Drug Products and the 9012 Guideline for Validation of Quantitative Analytical Method of Biological Samples, which were repeatedly included by the drug evaluation institution, were deleted. The 9104 Guideline for Near-Infrared Spectrophotometry has been deleted, and a new general provision, 0403 Near-Infrared Spectroscopy, has been added.<br>2 Main Features and Contents of amendments and revisions<br>2.1 General Requirements for Dosage Forms<br>0100 General Requirements for Dosage forms is a general monograph of different pharmaceutical preparations, dosage forms, and sub-dosage forms. In the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2015 Edition and the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition, the General Requirements for Dosage forms of traditional Chinese medicine, chemical drugs, and biological products were integrated, and the overall framework and technical requirements were systematically revised. In the General Requirements for Dosage forms of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, 23 kinds of dosage forms and sub-dosage forms such as tablets and injections have been improved, and their classifications and definitions have been further sorted out. The description of unless otherwise specified has been further clarified, and the relevant contents under the item of Production and Storage in the General Requirements for Dosage forms have been systematically sorted out. For 17 kinds of dosage forms, during the formulation determination and development stage, the research requirements for the antibacterial efficacy have been added. Attention has been paid to the inclusion of advanced technologies, methods, and requirements. The attention to the technical characteristics of relevant key preparations has been continuously strengthened. Coordinated with the formulation and revision of general physical and chemical analysis testing methods, the necessary testing items have been amended and revised; the coordination work between the relevant testing contents of the General Requirements for Dosage forms and the ICH guideline has been steadily and orderly promoted. It reflects the concept of the life cycle of preparations and provides necessary guidance for the improvement of the quality indicators of high-risk preparations.<br>2.1.1 Targeted amendments and revisions have been made to complex products<br>Examples are illustrated as follows.<br>0102 Injections. In coordination with ICH, after a comprehensive evaluation and study of the differences in judgment criteria, testing technical parameters, statistical parameters, etc., the part of [Fill Volume] in the test items has been revised.<br>0106 Nasal Preparations. In the production and storage section, relevant contents of the instructions for metered-dose nasal aerosols, metered-dose nasal sprays of suspension type and emulsion type, and multi-dose reservoir-type nasal powder sprays have been added. It is recommended that metered-dose nasal aerosols and metered-dose nasal sprays undergo tests of the spray pattern and spray morphology. In the test items, in order to ensure that nasal preparations can stably and uniformly deliver the labeled dose throughout their full life cycle, the determination methods and result judgments for the uniformity of delivered doses between bottles and within bottles have been revised. The tests requirements for the uniformity of delivered doses of single-dose packaged nasal preparations and nasal preparations with multiple active ingredients have been added.<br>0109 Ointments, Creams. The definitions of ointments and creams have been revised, and the descriptions of matrix classification, matrix components, composition, etc. have been improved and revised. Requirements for the evaluation and test of rheology, in vitro release, uniformity within the tube, and pH value, etc. have been newly added. According to whether the drug is in a dissolved or suspended state, requirements for the tests of crystallization or crystal form changes have been added.<br>0111 Preparations for Inhalation. The labeling requirements for inhalation aerosols, inhalation sprays, and inhalation powder sprays have been revised. The requirements for mass balance under the item of fine particle dose detection for inhalation aerosols, inhalation powder sprays, and inhalation sprays have been newly added.<br>0121 Patches. The definition of skin patches has been added, emphasizing that when used on severely damaged skin, it should be a sterile product. Considering that drug dosage is easily confused with delivered dose, it has been revised to drug amount, and total action time has been revised to application time. To adapt to the characteristics of the clinical use of transdermal patches, the specification description of transdermal patches has been added. At the same time, it is specified that the delivered dose of transdermal patches should be indicated on the label and/or instruction manual.<br>0125 Pellicles. The definition has been further improved. The pellicles have been classified, and the definitions of oral dissolving films, buccal films, sublingual films, and oral patches have been supplemented. The requirements during production and storage have also been improved. Requirements in terms of taste, irritation to the oral mucosa, moisture content, mechanical properties, microbial limits, dissolution rate, content uniformity, and residual solvents, etc., have been added. Quality requirements for the dissolution time limit, dissolution rate, and adhesiveness have been added.<br>2.1.2 Incorporating worldwide Pharmacopoeias and China's Industrial Development Trends, amendments and revisions to Guidelines The 9016 Guideline for Control of Visible Particles in Injections has been newly added. The visible foreign particles in injections are directly related to the safety of patients' medication, and they are an important quality indicator for injections. This guideline focuses on introducing the concept of controlling visible foreign particles based on the product full life cycle. It also elaborates on personnel training and inspection qualification confirmation related to the inspection method, inspection process confirmation, and inspection considerations for special injections. As a supplement to the Test for Visible Particles (0904), it aims to provide better guidance for the control of visible foreign particles in injections.<br>Tab. ？SEQ Table \* ARABIC 1 The revised list In the General Chapter 0931 Dissolution and Drug Release Test, determination methods such as the reciprocating frame method and the diffusion cell method have been newly added. Additionally, Near-infrared Spectrometry (0403), Atomic Fluorescence Spectrometry (0408), Scanning Electron Microscopy (0471), Determination of Acid-neutralizing Capacity (0953), Guideline for Rheology (9021), Guideline for Determination of Intrinsic Dissolution (9022), etc. have been newly added, so as to promote the application of scientific instruments independently developed in China in the pharmaceutical field.<br>Based on the application practice of instrumental analysis techniques and their development in recent years, General chapters such as Mass Spectrometry (0431), Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Spectrometry (0441), and X-ray Fluorescence Spectrometry (0461) have been revised. The contents and structures have been standardized, the application scopes have been further clarified, and necessary contents such as the description of principles and the requirements for instrument calibration have been improved.<br>2.3.2 Amend and revise the common requirements and testing items related to traditional Chinese medicine Scientific, reasonable, standardized and feasible technical reserves have been strengthened, general chapters related to traditional Chinese medicine preparations and methods for determination of biological activity of traditional Chinese medicine have been developed and revised, to provide effective support for further enhancing the leading role of traditional Chinese medicine standards, and improve the overall level of quality control of traditional Chinese medicine in China. The 9261 Guideline for Optically Stimulated Luminescence Testing of Irradiated Traditional Chinese Medicine has been newly added, and the 9305 Guideline for Determination of Mycotoxins in Traditional Chinese Medicine, 2341 Determination of Pesticide Residues, 2342 Determination of Plant Growth Regulator Residues, and 9303 Guideline for Establishment of Pigment Detection Method have been revised.<br>2.3.3 Practice the concept of green environmental protection and reduce the use of toxic and harmful reagents Alternative methods for the use of toxic and harmful reagents and test drugs have been studied and developed to reduce environmental pollution, personnel injuries, and experimental safety hazards. More scientific, reasonable, objective, and simple analytical and detection techniques have been included. In the third method of 0901 Colour of Solution, a method for comparing the color difference value of the test sample with the standard value of the determined standard colorimetric solution has been added to promote the popularization and application of objective, accurate, and digital detection methods; In 0902 Clarity of Solution, a new type of safe, non-toxic, and stable polymer microsphere turbidity standard solution has been newly added as a supplement to the formazin turbidity standard solution, which has potential carcinogenicity; In 0822 Limit Test for Arsenic, the atomic fluorescence spectrometry has been newly added as the third method, it replaces the use of arsenic standard solutions through instrumental detection and adds a method for preparing standard arsenic solutions with single-element arsenic standard solutions, solving the problems that arsenic trioxide reagent is highly toxic and difficult to purchase.<br>2.3.4 Amend and revise the process analytical technology requirements The application of 0403 Near-Infrared Spectroscopy and 0402 Infrared Spectroscopy in the quality control of new production processes such as continuous manufacturing has been amended. The 9031 Guideline for Chemical Imaging, 9095 Guideline for Multivariate Statistical Process Control Technology, and 9096 Guideline for Chemo-metrics have been amended. These guidelines provide instructions on model building, model selection, data processing methods, etc. in the development of process analytical technology methods, and lay a solid foundation for promoting the application of process analytical technology in the pharmaceutical industry in China.<br>2.3.5 Promote the application of statistics and metrology The technical requirements related to the life cycle of analytical methods and the standardization of analytical techniques have been developed and revised. The 9098 Guideline for Uncertainty Evaluation has been newly added, and the 9101 Guideline for Validation of Analytical Method has been revised. These are coordinated with ICH Q2 (R2) Validation of Analytical Procedures and Q14 Analytical Procedure Development, introducing new concepts and tools for analytical methods evaluation to improve the accuracy of product quality evaluation. The 9032 Guideline for Weighing with Analytical Electronic Balances has been newly added, and the 9201 Guideline for Validation of Alternative Microbiological Methods for Pharmaceutical Products, 1431 Statistical Methods for Biological Assays, 9097 Guideline for Interpretation and Processing of Analytical Data, and 9094 Guideline for Verification of Analytical Instruments have been revised, to strengthen the standardized management of laboratory analytical instruments, promote the application of statistics and metrology in the fields of drug research and development and quality control.<br>2.3.6 Add general test methods and guidelines According to the study results, the 0961 Dynamic Vapor Sorption has been newly added. The Dynamic Vapor Sorption (DVS) provides an effective means for studying the interaction between substances and vapors. By measuring the mass change of substances under different relative humidity conditions, it can accurately quantify the amount of solvent vapor adsorbed by the substances, and further evaluate the trend and degree of the adsorption or absorption of solvent vapor by the substances.
