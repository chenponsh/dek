| The introduction on the new standards of pharmaceutical excipients in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition | The Chinese Pharmacopoeia is an important part of the national drug standards, and it includes the technical requirements for drug research development, production (import), distribution, use and supervision. It not only includes the drug standards for traditional Chinese medicines, chemical drugs and biological products, but also the standards of pharmaceutical excipients and pharmaceutical packaging materials. The Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition has been obviously improved and perfected in terms of the quantity of standards for pharmaceutical excipients, standard system and standard formation mechanism . It has included 335 monographs (strengths) of pharmaceutical excipients. Although it has increased by 24% compared with the previous edition, it is far from covering all monographs (strengths) of pharmaceutical excipients in the marketed preparations .<br>According to the work goals and tasks determined by the outline of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, under the guidance of the national drug regulatory authorities, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission organized dozens of drug standard improvement project undertakers to conduct in-depth study, and after review by all members of the 12th Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission Specialized Committee of Pharmaceutical Excipients and invited experts, the development and revision of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition have been completed. Among them, 52 new pharmaceutical excipient standards have been added, and the total number has reached 387, representing an increase of 15.5% compared with the 2020 Edition.<br>This article focuses on the overview and key features of the new standards of pharmaceutical excipient monographs in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, to assist users of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia in understanding, implementing, and applying pharmacopoeia standards correctly. Relevant situations of 11 new pharmaceutical excipients included in the first supplement of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition have been introduced elsewhere, which are not covered here, and this article focuses on standards for 41 pharmaceutical excipients that are first included in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia.<br>1 Overview<br>The new standards of pharmaceutical excipients in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition embody several groups of key words:<br>The first is "Quantity and Quality Enhancement". The new standards of pharmaceutical excipients in this edition of Pharmacopoeia have significantly expanded the quantity, further narrowing the gap with the United States Pharmacopeia (USP), which includes over 500 monographs for pharmaceutical excipients. In addition, the newly added monographs cover a wide range of commonly used and specialized pharmaceutical excipients for traditional Chinese medicine, chemical drugs, and biological products. The standards emphasize critical quality attributes of pharmaceutical excipients, ensuring greater scientific rigor andstandardization in designation and testing requirements. The second is "Harmonization and Leadership". On the one hand, the standards enhance harmonization with relevant global and domestic standards, harmonization with standardsshared between drug substances and excipients , as well as standardization and harmonization with standards for multiple specifications under the same monograph and monographs of the same series; on the other hand, the ChP has taken a leadership role by being the first pharmacopoeia to include standards for several excipients, such as microcrystalline cellulose pellets, pregelatinized hydroxypropyl distarch phosphate, benzododecinium chloride, and pegylated phosphatidylethanolamine, and it also introduces a series of testing methods that are simpler,more accurate, and better aligned with green chemistry principles. <br>The third is "Supporting Role and Leading Role". The Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission has been adhering to a working model of "government guidance, enterprise leadership, and broad participation from across society", and encouraging active involvement in the formulation of standards of pharmaceutical excipients from pharmaceutical excipient manufacturers, users, testing institutions, and research organizations. Drug testing institutions, research institutes and universities, and manufacturing enterprises participate in the drafting of new standards of pharmaceutical excipients in this edition of Pharmacopoeia, thus fully leveraging the unique strengths ofall stakeholders. To enhance the quality and efficiency of standard development, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission introduced the "Guidance Unit" model, so as to offer precise assistance to manufacturing enterprises and first-time participants in pharmacopoeia standard development, and enable more technically capable organizations to take on leading roles in the pharmacopoeia standard development.<br>The new standards of pharmaceutical excipients in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition are provided in Table 1.<br>2 Main Characteristics of New Standards for Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>2.1 Oil-based Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>Oil-based excipients are a very common category of complex excipients characterized by diverse structures and significant analytical challenges. They include various mixtures, analogs, or derivatives of high-grade fatty acid glycerides. These excipients play versatile roles in drug delivery systems, including as solvents, lubricants, emulsifiers, bases for ointment or suppository, solubilizers, and stabilizers. They are commonly used in traditional dosage formulations and are increasingly important in advanced drug delivery systems such as liposomes and lipid emulsions, where they serve as carriers or delivery vehicles. Oil-based excipients have a wide range of applications, and immense potential for development, and high quality control requirements.<br>2.1.1 Sesame Oil as a pharmaceutical excipient, sesame oil is derived from the mature seeds of Sesamum indicum L. (family Pedaliaceae), and is a refined fatty oil composed primarily of various fatty acid glycerides. It is widely used in pharmaceutical formulations as a solvent or oil phase, with routes of administration including oral, injection, and topical dosage forms. Examples include its use in alfa-calcidol soft capsules and progesterone injections solutions. Standards for sesame oil have been included in the United States Pharmacopeia (hereinafter referred to as USP, including contents of NF), Japanese Pharmacopoeia (hereinafter referred to as JP) and Volume I of Chinese Pharmacopoeia, and it is included as "Refined Sesame Oil" in the European Pharmacopoeia (hereinafter referred to as EP). During the drafting process of the standards for pharmaceutical excipients standards, significant research has been conducted to improve the detection method for the glyceride composition of sesame oil. A comprehensive comparison was made between the normalization method in the USP and the linear external standard method in the EP, which uses an evaporative light scattering detector. The detection capabilities of conventional chromatographic columns were investigated to enhance the applicability of the method. The final established method can distinguish among 21 glyceride components in sesame oil.<br>2.1.2 Castor Oil as a pharmaceutical excipient, castor oil is derived from the mature seeds of Ricinus communis L. (family Euphorbiaceae), and is a refined fatty oil composed primarily of various fatty acid glycerides. It is unique among natural oils due to its high content (85%-92%) of ricinoleic acid, which contains a hydroxyl group, giving castor oil distinct solubility and viscosity properties. In pharmaceutical formulations, it is mainly used in topical creams and ointments at concentrations of 5%-12.5%, its high viscosity imparts sustained-release characteristics when it is used in injections and ophthalmic formulations, and it is also used in oral tablets and capsules. For example, it is used to delay the release of clopidogrel in coating formulations. Castor oil standards have been included in USP, EP, JP and Volume I of Chinese Pharmacopoeia. During the drafting process of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, a new method using a correction factor for ricinoleic acid normalization has been established, and qualitative and limit studies have been conducted on isoricinoleic acid, an isomer of ricinoleic acid. For the first time, a limit for the anisidine value (for injectable use) was introduced. In addition, in-depth studies have been performed on the ELSD normalization method for triglyceride composition included in USP, which was found to have limitations in its method and limit , and thus, this method was not adopted in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia.<br>Tab. 1 The new standards of pharmaceutical excipients in Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition<br>Name of Pharmaceutical oil is derived from the mature seeds of Helianthus annuus L. (family Asteraceae), is a refined fatty oil composed primarily of various fatty acid glycerides, with linoleic acid accounting for the highest proportion. Sunflower oil is primarily used as a solvent in pharmaceutical formulations, for example, it is used in progesterone soft capsules. Sunflower oil is included in both USP and EP. During the drafting of this standards, it is mainly focus on determination method of iodine value. Due to the high proportion of unsaturated fatty acids in sunflower oil, the reaction time for iodine value determination was extended in General Chapter 0713 (Fat and Fatty Oil Determination) in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, thus ensuring accuracy of test results. For fatty acid composition analysis, different concentrations of reference standards were prepared based on the content difference of major fatty acids in sunflower oil, to improve chromatographic separation.<br>2.1.4 Palm Oil Palm oil is mainly used as a coating agent and emulsifier in the drug product, is currently included in the USP, and enterprise standards vary. It is found in this standard study that there are two kinds of TLC identification tests in USP, method 1 and method 2, but the system suitability of method 2 is worse than that of method 1. Therefore, method 1, which is more accurate and feasible, is finally adopted. In the study of fatty acid composition analysis, it was found that linolenic acid contains -linolenic acid and -linolenic acid, both of which may exist in nature. Therefore, the type of linolenic acid is specified in the draft standards, making the standards more perfect and rigorous.<br>2.1.5 Hydrogenated Palm Oil Hydrogenated palm oil is a hydrogenated vegetable oil obtained by refining and hydrogenation of palm oil, is mainly composed of triglycerides containing palmitic acid and stearic acid, and is commonly used in suppositories, capsules, tablets, ointments, etc. Currently, it is only included in the United States Pharmacopeia (USP), and enterprise standards vary. In this standard study, appropriate standards have been established in combination with the manufacturing process of the product, which can promote in-depth research on the application of this pharmaceutical excipient.<br>2.1.6 Hydrogenated Vegetable Oil Hydrogenated vegetable oil is prepared by refining, bleaching, deodorizing, and spray drying of oil of vegetable origin, mainly containing triglycerides. Common vegetable oil sources are palm oil, soybean oil, cottonseed oil, etc. Hydrogenated vegetable oil can be used in the formulation of lipophilic controlled-release drug delivery systems and as a viscosity modifier in oil-based liquid and semi-solid formulations. hydrogenated vegetable oil have been included in USP and JP, and standards for monographs of hydrogenated soybean oil, hydrogenated cottonseed oil, and hydrogenated palm oil have been separately included in the pharmacopoeia of many countries. During the drafting of the standards, the standards of hydrogenated vegetable oil monographs in various national pharmacopoeias were taken into account, and the routine quality control items for lipids such as acid value, iodine value and saponification value of hydrogenated vegetable oil were developed for the first time; At the same time, the residue of catalyst nickel in hydrogenation process of lipid was studied, and the method and limit were established. In addition, considering the special melting point of hydrogenated vegetable oil, and due to the possibility of coagulation and agglomeration during the testing of unsaponifiable substances, acid value and loss on drying, the operational details were specified in the standards.<br>2.1.7 Tetradecyl Alcohol Tetradecyl alcohol, also known as myristol alcohol, is extracted from vegetable oils containing tetradecyl ester, such as coconut oil, palm kernel oil, etc., and is prepared by hydrogenation reduction under catalytic conditions. It is mainly used as a softener and stabilizer, etc. In this standard study, the general quality control items for lipid-based excipients were formulated in combination with the structural characteristics of the product, including acid value, hydroxyl value, iodine value, saponification value, water content, etc., and the items of related substances and assay were mainly studied. The product has been included in USP. However, in the study, it was found that the column used in the USP method did not perform well, and there was serious column bleeding when the temperature was higher than 180C, resulting in an uneven baseline in the chromatogram, which affected the detection of related substances. Therefore, the gas chromatography using polyethylene glycol was established to determine the related substances and assay of the product by screening columns with different packing and optimizing the chromatographic conditions.<br>2.1.8 Oleic Acid Oleic acid is purified and refined from fats and oils derived from vegetable. It contains saturated and unsaturated fatty acids in different contents, with the main component being cis-9-octadecenoic acid. It can be used as an emulsifier in fat emulsion injections (such as medium/long chain fat emulsion injection and propofol medium/long chain fat emulsion injection) to form a stable oil/water system. It is also often used as an emulsifier, transdermal enhancer or soft capsule matrix in topical formulations or oral formulations to improve the bioavailability of poorly soluble drugs, etc. This product is registered as "Oleic Acid" or "Oleic Acid (for Injection)" on the CDE registration platform of APIs, pharmaceutical excipients and packaging materials, and is included in both the USP and EP. The domestic standards include the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 1953 Edition, registration standards and enterprise standards, which are very different and difficult to coordinate. During the process of drafting the standards for pharmaceutical excipients, more than twenty quality control items such as residual solvents, elemental impurities, fatty acid composition, and bacterial endotoxins were investigated based on domestic and international standards as well as the production situations and application status of Oleic acid, and in order to make the standard items comprehensive but not redundant, more than ten items such as acid value, iodine value, peroxide value, fatty acid composition, and assay were finally determined to be included, and reasonable limits were set. Considering that oleic acid as a pharmaceutical excipient has a wide application range and a large span of content limits, an appropriate way of expressing the assay limit is set in the standard, that is, "the oleic acid content shall not be less than 65.0% and shall not be less than the labeled percentage content", and enterprises are required to clearly state the percentage content of oleic acid in this product in the labeling item. This way not only reflects the minimum requirement for the oleic acid content, but also helps enterprises to distinguish and select the appropriate purity specifications.<br>2.1.9 Tricaprylin Tricaprylin is obtained by the esterification of caprylic acid and glycerol. It is often used as a lubricant, emulsifier and pharmaceutical carrier. At the same time, it is suitable for the preparation of multivesicular liposomes, playing the roles of filling gaps, preventing membrane fusion and maintaining the stability of the honeycomb-like multi-compartment structure. It is increasingly used in liposome-related marketed products and in the research and development stage. This product is included in the USP. In addition to formulating general quality control items for oil-based excipients such as acid value, hydroxyl value, peroxide value, saponification value and water content, this standard has also studied and established two gas chromatography methods for the determination of the content of glycerides and fatty acid composition respectively. In addition, considering that the redox titration method used in the USP for the determination of free glycerol in this product has poor specificity, a more accurate and feasible size exclusion chromatography method has been established for the first time [.<br>2.1.10 Ascorbyl Palmitate Ascorbyl palmitate is obtained by the esterification of palmitic acid and L-ascorbic acid. It is commonly used in oral and topical formulations as an antioxidant for drugs that are unstable to oxygen. The combination of ascorbyl palmitate and -tocopherol shows a significant synergistic effect. During the process of drafting the standards of Ascorbyl Palmitate, it was found that the samples are extremely prone to being oxidized by air during storage, resulting in a darker color. Therefore, attention should be paid to the storage conditions. Regarding the assay, EP uses the titration method, and the USP uses the high performance liquid chromatography method. After comparison and analysis, the high performance liquid chromatography method with better sensitivity and specificity is included in the standard as the assay method.<br>2.1.11 Polyoxyethylene (15) Hydroxystearate Polyoxyethylene (15) hydroxystearate is a mixture obtained by the polycondensation of ethylene oxide and 12-hydroxystearic acid in a ratio of 15:1. The main components include the monoester of polyethylene glycol and 12-hydroxystearic acid, the diester of polyethylene glycol and 12-hydroxystearic acid, the polyethylene glycol ether of 12-hydroxystearic acid, as well as free polyethylene glycol. Currently, the name registered on the CDE (Center for Drug Evaluation) registration platform of APIs, pharmaceutical excipients and packaging materials is "Polyethylene Glycol 15-Hydroxystearate". Considering that this name may be ambiguous, it is named Polyoxyethylene (15) Hydroxystearate with reference to the Nomenclature Principles for Generic Names of Pharmaceutical Excipients in China. As a new type of non-ionic solubilizer and emulsifier, Polyoxyethylene (15) Hydroxystearate exhibits good safety in the applications of oral capsules, injections, emulsions for injection, ophthalmic preparations, and nasal drops, etc. Moreover, it can promote drug absorption and improve bioavailability. When conducting experiments with reference to the Determination of Fats and Fatty Oils (General Chapter 0713) in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, it was found that in the blank test, after titrating the solution to a pale yellow color with sodium thiosulfate and then adding the starch indicator, the solution turned blue. However, when the sample was present, the addition of the starch indicator did not cause the solution to turn blue. It is speculated that this is due to the inclusion effect of the sample on the generated iodine. Therefore, in the standards formulated this time, the end point of titration is determined according to the color change of the solution. According to the characteristics of the process, a specific detection method for controlling the limits of ethylene oxide and dioxane of this product has been established. Nickel or sodium hydroxide is added as a catalyst during the synthesis process of this product, and the nickel content of some samples exceeded the control threshold (30% of the limit). To meet the requirements for the safety and efficacy of the product, the risk of the elemental impurity nickel is indicated in the "Note" of the standard, and a detection method is provided.<br>2.2 Starch-based Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>Starch is not only an important source of food for human beings, but also has a history of more than 100 years as a pharmaceutical excipient, and it is a traditional bulk pharmaceutical excipient. Starch-based pharmaceutical excipients include native starch and modified starch, which are commonly used as fillers, binders, disintegrants, etc. in traditional dosage forms such as tablets, capsules, and granules. The application of native starch in drug products is greatly influenced by its original properties. In order to improve its application performance or expand new applications in drug products, based on the inherent characteristics of native starch, a series of modified starches are prepared through one or more processing methods such as physical, chemical, enzymatic or fermentation methods, making them more suitable for the application function requirements of drug products. Starch-based excipients have good biocompatibility and biodegradability. With the continuous development of starch processing technology and the improvement of the application requirements of formulations, modified starches are widely used in new drug delivery systems. They show good expectations in aspects such as thermal property and pH-dependent drug delivery models, nanoparticles, gels, etc., which endows starch-based pharmaceutical excipients with great development potential.<br>2.2.1 Hydroxypropyl Starch Hydroxypropyl starch is the product of etherification of some or all of the free hydroxyl groups in starch with hydroxypropyl groups. It has good stability and film-forming properties, and is mainly used as a binder and filler. Due to different sources of starch, there are different quality requirements. Both the USP and the EP include it, but the ways of inclusion are different. The USP includes the products prepared from different starch sources as different monographs respectively; the EP includes the products from different starch sources as the same monograph, but sets different requirements for products from different starch sources under the testing items. The Chinese Pharmacopoeia has adopted the approach of the EP. This time, the study has been focused on the analytical methods of the genotoxic impurities 1-chloro-2-propanol and 2-chloro-1-propanol that may be generated in the process. For the first time, a gas chromatography-mass spectrometry method with good sensitivity and specificity has been established to reduce the interference of samples.<br>2.2.2 Pregelatinized Hydroxypropyl Distarch Phosphate Pregelatinized hydroxypropyl distarch phosphate is prepared by subjecting starch to etherification and cross-linking reactions under alkaline conditions, followed by treatments such as washing, filtration, drying, gelatinization, and crushing. Hydroxypropyl distarch phosphate modified by hydroxypropyl groups and cross-linking shows remarkable improvements in shear resistance and acid resistance, and its transparency and freeze-thaw stability are enhanced. The product can be used as a thickener, binder, etc. Although it is not included in the pharmacopoeias of various countries, it has a long application history in pharmaceuticals. In this standard research, references were made to starch-based monographs in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, USP "Modified Starch" and "Pregelatinized Modified Starch", GB29931-2013 Food Additive Hydroxypropyl Distarch Phosphate and other standards. Combined with the production process and application scenarios, in-depth studies were carried out on its inorganic impurities and organic impurities. According to the properties of this product, the analytical methods for the genotoxic impurities 1-chloro-2-propanol and 2-chloro-1-propanol were optimized to improve the specificity and detection sensitivity[9]. <br>2.2.3 Sulfobutylbetacyclodextrin Sodium Sulfobutylbetacyclodextrin sodium is mainly used as a solubilizer and stabilizer. Currently, it has been applied in a variety of marketed antiepileptic drugs, antifungal drugs, antiviral drugs, etc. For example, remdesivir, which has been approved for the treatment of severe COVID-19, also contains it. Both the USP and the EP have included this excipient while there are significant differences in the analytical methods of key quality control indicators, such as the average degree of substitution, sodium 4-hydroxybutane-1-sulfonate, disodium bis(4-sulfobutyl) ether, etc. In this standard research, the sensitivity, accuracy, and applicability of relevant methods were mainly compared with.actual use requirements been considered, the standards were scientifically formulated.<br>2.2.4 L-Lactic acid Lactic acid is usually prepared from starch raw materials, such as corn, rice, dried potato, etc., converted into carbohydrates after treatment and then fermented. It is a mixture of 2-hydroxypropionic acid and its condensates with optical activity. It is difficult to metabolize D-lactic acid in the human body. All raw materials and excipients used in the field of medicine are L-lactic acid. As a pharmaceutical excipient, this product is often used as pH adjusting agent, cosolvent, etc. and can be used in injections, oral preparations and topical preparations. EP lists the standards for lactic acid (racemic) and (S)-lactic acid. JP lists the standards for lactic acid and L-lactic acid, respectively. USP lists the standard of lactic acid, and the definition indicated there are two kinds of lactic acid: racemic and levorotatory, with levorotatory lactic acid obtained from carbohydrates fermentation and racemic lactic acid from synthesis. The standard for lactic acid (API) listed in Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition does not yet specify or classify this aspect. In this research on the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, the optical rotation and preparation method of the lactic acid were described according to the product characteristics in actual use. Specific rotation determination was added to control the product to be levorotatory, and infrared identification was included to improve the specificity of identification. Additionally, tests for lactic acid monomers, related substances, and ether-insoluble matter were added according to the manufacturing process of this lactic acid.<br>2.3 Gel-based Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>2.3.1 Carrageenan Carrageenan is a natural sulfated polysaccharide polymer extracted from red algae such as Chondrus spp., Pelvetia spp., and Eucheuma spp. It is the interstitial matrix between the cellulose fibers in the tissue of red algae. Carrageenan has good gelling, thickening, emulsifying and film-forming properties, and is used as a pharmaceutical excipient in various non-injectable dosage forms, including suspensions, topical gels, eye drops, suppositories, tablets, capsules, and microsphere carrier materials. Different types of carrageenan products can be extracted from different raw materials. The commonly used carrageenan types are , and . These three types of carrageenan have different colloidal properties due to different positions and quantities of sulfate ester substitutions. In the drafting of this standard, researchers mainly focused the classification of carrageenan and the establishment of functional items. The type of carrageenan can be effectively identified through the combination of infrared identification and chemical identification. The analytical method of functional items such as viscosity, gel strength, and sulfate ester were evaluated and listed under the standard.<br>2.3.2 Gelatin Gelatin is a protein obtained from moderate hydrolysis and thermal denaturation of collagen in animal connective tissue. It has excellent physical and chemical properties as well as biocompatibility. Therefore, it has been widely used in the field of pharmaceutical excipients. The USP, EP, and JP list the standards for gelatin. Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia lists the standard for gelatin used in hemostatic sponges, and Volume IV of Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition lists the standard for gelatin used in capsules. In this research on the standards of gelatin as pharmaceutical excipients, the low specificity of existing identification method was solved. The newly established method can detect hydroxyproline, a characteristic amino acid of collagen, thus effectively distinguishing gelatin from other proteins. The standards cover non-gelling gelatin, broadening the scope of compendial standards. With consideration of manufacturing process, test methods and limits of iron and zinc were established.<br>2.4 Polymeric Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>2.4.1 Povidone series Povidone is a synthetic polymer obtained by the polymerization reaction of N-vinylpyrrolidone. It belongs to a non-ionic water-soluble macromolecular compound, has excellent physiological inertness and biocompatibility, and is widely used. Together with cellulose derivatives and acrylic compounds, it is known as one of the three major synthetic pharmaceutical excipients. It is often used as an adhesive, cosolvent, solid dispersion carrier and pore-forming agent in sustained and controlled-release preparations. Volume IV of Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition only lists povidone K30. The USP, EP and JP list one variety of povidone. Only EP subdivides the K value of povidone into K12, K17, K25, K30, and K90. The 2025 edition of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia adds K25 and K90. Due to different reaction mechanisms, the measured values of 2-pyrrolidone in povidone K25, K30 and K90 are different. The standard in ChP 2025 established the different limits of 2-pyrrolidone according to the manufacturing process of povidone in different varieties for the first time. International harmonization of standards was emphasized in this research and elemental impurities were evaluated according to the concept of ICH Q3D. At the same time, the test method of formic acid in povidone K90, which often showed poor separation in foreign pharmacopoeias, was optimized to improve operability.<br>2.4.2 Acrylate copolymers Acrylate copolymers are a class of macromolecular polymers copolymerized from acrylic acid, methacrylic acid and their esters in different proportions, which have the advantages of safety, stability, inertness and non-stimulation. The polymerization reactions include bulk polymerization, solution polymerization, inverse emulsion polymerization and inverse suspension polymerization. Different monomers can be polymerized into polymers with different properties, and are often used as film coating and drug controlled-release materials. These acrylate copolymers are listed in the USP and EP, but the listed forms are slightly different: e.g., for the copolymer of methacrylic acid-methyl methacrylate, the USP lists one variety, and EP divides it into two varieties according to different monomer content. In addition, the copolymers of methacrylic acid-methyl methacrylate (1:1) and (1:2), as well as the copolymer of aminoalkyl methacrylate, are very similar to the polyacrylic resin Ⅱ, Ⅲ and Ⅳ listed in the current edition of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, but the polymerization process is different. The former is the emulsion polymerization process, and the latter is the solvent polymerization process. Due to different polymerization processes, there are some differences in viscosity, molecular weight distribution, etc. If the former one directly replaces the latter which already used in the market, it may cause problems in the related drug products. In order to provide more excipients for the research and development of the drug product, the copolymers of methacrylic acid-methyl methacrylate (1:1) and (1:2) and the copolymer of aminoalkyl methacrylate were newly added this time. At the same time, the standards for the copolymer of methacrylic acid-ethyl acrylate and its aqueous dispersion, and some partially neutralized monographs were also included. In this research on the standards, the infrared identification, viscosity, residual monomers, elemental impurities and monomer contents were mainly studied in combination with the characteristics of acrylate copolymers. The infrared absorption reference spectra of each variety are attached to facilitate identification and comparison. The residual monomers are controlled to ensure the safety of product usage. The limit of residue on ignition were properly set to better meet the requirements in actual use. As some acrylic copolymers will be added with surfactant, which may have certain effect on the drug product, the name and dosage of surfactant shall be clearly indicated in the item.<br>2.5 Pellet Core Pharmaceutical Excipients - Microcrystalline Cellulose Pellet Cores<br>The microcrystalline cellulose pellet cores are prepared by granulating microcrystalline cellulose with water and are mainly used as core material carrier materials for gastroenteric micro-pellet preparations and sustained and controlled-release preparations. Compared with the commonly used sucrose pellet cores, microcrystalline cellulose pellet cores have higher strength and hardness, and also have the advantage of sugar-free clinical application. The product is widely used in the marketed drug products. Currently, this standard is not listed in Chinese and foreign pharmacopoeias . Imported products of microcrystalline cellulose pellet cores shall be subject to their import registration specification, while generally domestic products shall be subject to the specification issued by the relevant authorities or internal control standards set by individual enterprises. The safety indicators in the specification for this product are basically the same as those of its raw material, microcrystalline cellulose. Particle size is an important functional indicator of the product. Since the particle size of the product is usually customized according to the client's requirements, and there are many particle size specifications of the product, the particle size of products of different specifications shall be distinguished by their labeled particle size. Therefore, it is required in the standard labeling item to indicate the particle size range of the pellet cores. During the drafting process, the functional indicators affecting the application of the product such as particle shape, and surface concavity and convexity of the particles were also studied. However, these indicators were not reflected in the sepcification temporarily due to the maturity of the product and method application.<br>2.6 Gaseous Pharmaceutical Excipients - Nitrogen<br>Nitrogen is chemically stable and difficult to react with other substances at room temperature. Due to its chemical inertness, it is commonly used as a protective gas to prevent oxidation of certain materials when exposed to air. In the pharmaceutical industry, nitrogen is widely used as an air displacer for stability of medicinal products. Nitrogen is listed in EP, USP, and JP. In the studies on the specification, the suggestions of the manufacturing enterprises and the preparation application enterprises were collected extensively by means of online questionnaires, on-site discussions, letters, and phone communications, etc. In-depth investigation was conducted on the production process, application scenarios, and key quality attributes of this product. For the first time, the products from two commonly used preparation process sources, namely cryogenic method and molecular sieving method, were listed in the specification, and the appropriate water and oxygen limits were formulated. Gas chromatography is used to determine the nitrogen content (nitrogen content = 100 - oxygen content) in foreign pharmacopoeias, and the chromatographic conditions are basically the same, but under the chromatographic conditions, except for oxygen, other impurities such as moisture, carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide cannot be detected. In the drafting of this specification, the determination of oxygen in the test sample by micro-oxygen analyzer was established with good specificity and accuracy and high sensitivity. The nitrogen content was calculated by mass balance method to cover impurities such as oxygen, moisture, carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide. The content determination results were more accurate and the method was simple and convenient.<br>2.7 Amino Acid-based Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>2.7.1 Arginine hydrochloride Arginine hydrochloride is an amino acid with significant physiological activity and can be used as a protecting agent, a stabilizing agent, and a pH adjusting agent in the drug product. It is listed in USP, EP, BP, and Volume II of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, but the listed items, methods, or limits are different. For the control of trace insoluble impurities and colored substances, the light transmittance test is used in Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia, and the clarity and color test is used in EP and JP. During the drafting process of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, these two methods were compared, and a more intuitive light transmittance test method was selected to avoid interference of human factors on the determination results. The other amino acids in the sample were investigated by thin layer chromatography (TLC) and amino acid analyzer respectively, showing no significant difference between the two methods. In comparison, the amino acid analyzer has high precision and repeatability and sensitivity. However, considering that the amino acid analyzer is relatively expensive, TLC is still used in the specification. In addition, the non-aqueous titration assay with mercury acetate as a masking agent was modified. This not only avoids the use of the toxic reagent mercury acetate, but also improves the precision, stability and accuracy of the method.<br>2.7.2 Histidine Hydrochloride Histidine hydrochloride is produced by microbial fermentation or protein hydrolysis and obtained through processing and refining. It is the main raw material for various amino acid-containing nutritional preparations. With the rapid development of biopharmaceuticals, histidine and its hydrochloride salt, due to their unique structures and properties, are widely used in the form of pharmaceutical excipients in the production processes and formulation preparations of biological products such as recombinant proteins, antibodies, vaccines, and small-molecule nucleic acids, mainly serving as buffers, antioxidants, and stabilizers. Histidine hydrochloride has been included in the EP, JP, and the Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia. During the drafting process of the specification of pharmaceutical excipients, the chromatographic conditions for the tests of other amino acids were mainly optimized, and the problem of chloride ions interfering with the main spot of histidine was solved; the range of specific rotation was tightened; a systematic study was conducted on weakly absorbing impurities, biogenic amine impurities, isomers, residual proteins, residual nucleic acids, elemental impurities, etc. According to the actual quality status of the samples, the tests included in the specification were optimized.<br>2.8 Antibacterial Pharmaceutical Excipients<br>2.8.1 Benzododecinium Chloride Benzododecinium chloride is a single component of dimethylbenzylammonium chloride substituted by n-C12H25, and is mainly prepared by the condensation of dodecyldimethyl tertiary amine and benzyl chloride. This product is a quaternary ammonium salt cationic surfactant with bactericidal effect, and is a commonly used bacteriostatic agent in ophthalmic preparations. Currently, pharmacopoeias of various countries have only included the homologous mixture benzalkonium chloride, and have not separately included benzododecinium chloride. During the study of this specification, horizontal harmonization was carried out with reference to the specification of benzalkonium chloride and benzalkonium bromide, and an "alkyl homologues and other impurities item" with stronger resolution was set up. According to the characteristics of the synthesis process of this product, tests for organic impurities such as benzyl alcohol, benzaldehyde, and benzyl chloride were set up in the specification.<br>2.8.2 Sodium Ethylparaben Sodium ethylparaben, also known as sodium ethyl p-hydroxybenzoate, is refined by adding ethyl p-hydroxybenzoate (ethylparaben) to an aqueous solution of sodium hydroxide and allowing the reaction to proceed. This product has the antibacterial performance and safety performance of parabens, and at the same time overcomes the defect of low water solubility of parabens. It is often used as a bacteriostatic agent in oral, topical, and local preparations, and is also widely used for antibacterial purposes in the pharmaceutical, food, and textile industries, as well as in other fields such as cosmetics, feeds, and daily industrial products. This time, by comparing the USP and GB specifications and combining with the production process of this product, the test method was optimized, and appropriate control items were established for its inorganic impurities and organic process impurities.<br>2.8.3 Phenol As a commonly used pharmaceutical excipient, phenol also plays a role in inhibiting bacteria and preventing corruption in preparations. It is often used in injections such as insulin and oxytocin, as well as in traditional Chinese medicine preparations and topical preparations. During the drafting of the specifications of pharmaceutical excipients, by comparing the standards of the USP, EP, JP, and the Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia, and combining with the production process of this product, items such as acidity, clarity and color, water content, non-volatile substances, and content were controlled. During the drafting process, volatile impurities in phenol, such as cumene, acetone, methanol, anisole, methyl isobutyl ketone, and benzene, were all investigatedAnd in accordance with the relevant principles of the harmonization between the pharmaceutical excipients in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia and ICH Q3C？？, the corresponding items were not set in the specifications.<br>2.9 Animal Origin<br>2.9.1 Squalene Squalene has an immune activation effect, and is used in oil-in-water emulsion adjuvants such as MF59 and AS03. Among them, MF59 has been used in the trivalent influenza vaccine Fluad and the quadrivalent influenza vaccine Fluad Quadrivalent, and has been demonstrated to enhance the immune response; AS03 has been used in H1N1 and H5N1 vaccines. This product is included in the EP. During the drafting process of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, considering that this product is a polyene product, a detailed study was carried out on the operation details of the iodine value detection test, and finally, iodine monochloride with higher reaction activity was selected for use in the reaction. Moreover, the test methods for bacterial endotoxins and microorganisms of this product were developed and studied to make them meet the needs of biological products.<br>2.9.2 Protamine Sulfate As a pharmaceutical excipient, protamine sulfate is mainly used as a complexing agent. It is a sulfate of a group of basic polypeptides extracted from the fresh and mature sperm of salmonid fish. This product is included as a drug in the EP, USP, and the Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia. Currently, the quality control in the latter is relatively simple. The existing identification methods cannot distinguish fish species from different sources, and no effective physical and chemical methods are included to control the content and related substances. During the research on the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, the high performance liquid chromatography method was established with reference to the USP for fish species identification, and at the same time, the content of protamine sulfate and related substances were determined. The method of using the animal whole blood or plasma for determination was replaced with more stable instrumental analytical method, solving the problem of large experimental errors in the animal method . In addition, liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry technology was used to identify the amino acid sequences of the four main peptide segments. Standard chromatograms and the amino acid sequences of the four peptide segments are attached to the standard for reference during use. At the same time, considering the use of organic solvents in the production process, a method for determining residual solvents was established and given in the form of a recommended method for convenient use when needed.<br>2.10 Other<br>2.10.1 Calcium Carbonate Calcium carbonate is a monograph with both medicinal and excipient uses. As a pharmaceutical excipient, it is mainly used as a diluent for solid preparations. In dispersible tablets, it acts as an auxiliary agent for buffering and dispersion. It can also be used as a coating material in the coating process of sugar-coated tablets and as an opacifier for film-coated tablets. According to different processing methods, calcium carbonate can be divided into light calcium carbonate and heavy calcium carbonate. Light calcium carbonate is obtained through chemical reactions, while heavy calcium carbonate is obtained by physically grinding natural minerals. During the drafting of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, elemental impurities and functional items were comprehensively investigated. The tests for fluoride and lead were added, and the test method for barium salts was optimized. At the same time, the process and classification of calcium carbonate were prompted, and it was required to indicate the type of the product (heavy or light), the particle size and particle size distribution, and the marked values of packing density and tapped density for the pharmaceutical enterprises to select.<br>2.10.2 Mannitol This product can be used both as drug and excipient. As a pharmaceutical excipient, due to its low toxic and side effects, good biocompatibility, non-hygroscopicity, and good taste, it is used as a filler, lyophilization protectant, flavoring agent, etc. The mannitol standards included in the USP, EP, and BP are applicable to all crystal forms of mannitol (, , , and mixed crystals). The mannitol standards in the Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition are only applicable to the crystal form. However, the non- crystal form mannitol has great application potential in pharmaceutical preparations due to its unique physical and chemical properties. In the establishment of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, a crystal transformation method was established, enabling the standards to cover all crystal forms of mannitol. Considering the usage routes, the microbial limits and bacterial endotoxin control were carried out in a targeted manner. The indication of the key functional indicator of particle size was added to facilitate the selection of appropriate excipients by pharmaceutical manufacturers. The process impurities of mannitol were further confirmed. The assay method was optimized to improve its specificity. Nickel or sodium hydroxide is added as a catalyst during the synthesis of this product. To meet the requirements of the safety and efficacy of the preparations, the risk of elemental impurity nickel was indicated in the "Note" of the standard, and a detection method was provided.<br>2.10.3 Zinc Chloride Zinc chloride is commonly used as a stabilizer in insulin injections, and can also slow down the release of insulin. It can also be used as a stabilizer and anti-allergic agent in ophthalmic preparations. This product is included in USP, EP and JP. It is a small molecule compound of inorganic mineral origin and is mainly used in injections, so the risk of elemental impurities is the main concern. Through a comprehensive assessment, it was found that lead element in this product posed a risk. Therefore, the tests for lead was set in the standard, and atomic absorption spectrophotometry was used. The tests for elements such as aluminum, calcium, iron and magnesium were set, and a more convenient chemical reaction method was used. The risk of cadmium element was relatively low, and was prompted under the "Note" item.<br>2.10.4 Glycopyrronium aluminum Glycopyrronium aluminum includes the anhydrous and hydrated forms of aluminum dihydroxyglycinate, which can be used as an adhesive for gel matrices in plaster products such as flurbiprofen gel plasters. Since this product is an organic complex with poor water solubility, it is difficult to remove substances that are insoluble in water. The raw materials or by-products of the synthesis process (such as inorganic substances like aluminum hydroxide) may remain in the final product. Therefore, effectively controlling the related substances of this product is of great importance. Currently, the synthesis method used for this product both domestically and internationally is the aluminum isopropoxide synthesis method. Aluminum isopropoxide is synthesized from isopropanol and aluminum under the catalysis of mercury chloride. Therefore, the specification includes the content of isopropanol, chlorides, and mercury in this product. In addition, since the assay item detects aluminum through titration method, the presence of impurities such as aluminum hydroxide can affect the results, and the assay results cannot reflect the product purity. Therefore, a nitrogen content test is set up to further control the amount of nitrogen-containing substances, achieving the control of the purity and content of this product.<br>2.10.5 Tributyl Phosphate Tributyl phosphate is esterified from n-butanol and phosphorus oxychloride, and can be used as a solvent or plasticizer in injections. During the drafting process of this standard, a comparative study was conducted on the acid value determination methods in Chinese and foreign pharmacopoeias to determine more operable experimental conditions. A systematic evaluation was carried out on elemental impurities. The lead element exceeded the control threshold value of elemental impurities in injections but did not exceed the limit. Therefore, control requirements were added under the "Note" item in the standard.<br>2.10.6 Benzyl Benzoate Benzyl benzoate is a product that can be used as drug or excipient. As a pharmaceutical excipient, it is mainly used as a cosolvent. It is included in USP, EP, and JP. The related substances are controlled by physical and chemical tests such as acidity and aldehydes in these pharmacopoeias, but the specificity of these methods is limited. The assay methods are all acid-base titration method. During the drafting process of the standards of pharmaceutical excipients, a high performance liquid chromatography method with stronger specificity and higher sensitivity was developed. This method can simultaneously detect process and degradation impurities such as benzyl alcohol, benzyl chloride, benzoic acid, and benzaldehyde that may be introduced into benzyl benzoate, and can also be used for assay.<br>2.10.7 Water for injection Water for injection was originally included in the Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia. Considering that it is used in the drug manufacturing process or as a pharmaceutical excipient, it is no longer included in the Volume II of Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition and is included in the Volume IV instead. The preparation method and testing items have also been revised.<br>The Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition has not only significantly increased the number of new monographs but also systematically revised the standards of pharmaceutical excipients in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition. The specific details will be described in another article.
