| The introduction on the standards system of water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition | Water for pharmaceutical purposes is used widely and extensively in the production process of medicines and the preparation of drug products, and its quality control is of vital importance for ensuring drug safety. Serial standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes have been included in the current edition of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia. Based on industry demands and the preliminary research progress, the edition outline of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition has proposed "following international advanced technologies and concepts concerning water for pharmaceutical purposes to propel the improvement of China's standards system of water for pharmaceutical purposes". The Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission has organized systematic revision and improvement of the standards system of water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, involving relevant general chapters, general testing methods, guidelines and monographs in volume II and volume IV of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia. This article focuses on the work background, overall approach, work methodology, standard framework, key content and significance on the standards system of water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, with the aim of assisting users in correctly understanding, implementing, or utilizing the standards in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia.<br>1 Work background for the formulation and revision of standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia<br>1.1 Incorporation of standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition<br>General Chapter "0261 Water for Pharmaceutical Purposes" in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2020 Edition (Volume IV) specifies that "In this edition of the Pharmacopoeia of the People's Republic of China, water for pharmaceutical purposes includes drinking water, purified water, water for injection and sterile water for injection according to the purposes for which it is to be used." Additionally, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia (Volume IV) incorporates two relevant general testing methods, i.e., "0681 Determination of Conductivity of Water for Pharmaceutical Use" and "0682 Determination of Total Organic Carbon in Water for Pharmaceutical Use".<br>The Chinese Pharmacopoeia (Volume II) incorporates three monographs: Purified Water, Water for Injection and Sterile Water for Injection. Purified Water is also incorporated in Volume IV, with a note "See the same monograph in Volume II".<br>1.2 Main opinions and suggestions previously collected<br>In recent years, relevant manufacturers, associations and experts have consistently raised requests regarding general chapters and monographs for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia. These requests primarily include the following: (1) Pharmacopoeias such as the European Pharmacopoeia (EP) and the United States Pharmacopoeia (USP) and relevant WHO standards have already permitted the preparation of water for injection by non-distillation methods, while both General Chapter 0261 and the monograph of Water for Injection in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia state "Water for injection is prepared by distillation of purified water". It is hoped that this restriction may be removed from the Chinese Pharmacopoeia. (2) The monographs incorporate an excessive number of testing items, some of which are redundant and time-consuming and have been removed from pharmacopoeias such as the EP and the USP for years. It is hoped that the testing items in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia may be optimized. (3) Pharmacopoeias of other countries have incorporated multiple types of finished water such as sterile purified water, sterile water for inhalation and sterile water for irrigation, which can effectively reduce production and control costs for manufacturers. It is hoped that the Chinese Pharmacopoeia may also incorporate these types of water. In addition, some experts hope that the Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission can centrally manage, comprehensively consider and collaboratively promote the formulation of the standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes; manufacturers generally lack concepts and methods for controlling contaminants such as microorganisms, particulate matters and elemental impurities in water for pharmaceutical purposes, and there is an urgent need for the Chinese Pharmacopoeia to provide stronger guidance in this regard.<br>2 Overall approach and work methodology for the establishment of the standards system of water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition<br>In view of the extensive concern about and the great influence of the standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia Commission carried out the work following the principles of seeking progress while maintaining stability, promoting stability through progress, taking all aspects into consideration, collaborating with relevant parties to make progress, fully implementing the main responsibilities of manufacturers and strengthening the concepts of risk management and whole process management.<br>After multiple discussions with relevant affiliated organizations, manufacturers and associations, it was determined that the revision of the standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition would focus on three main issues: I. Collaborating with relevant parties to study the feasibility of introducing non-distillation methods for preparing water for injection into relevant standards in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia (led by China Pharmaceutical Association of Plant Engineering (CPAPE) and participated by RD-Based Pharmaceutical Association Committee (RDPAC) and Jiangsu Pharmaceutical Association); II. Adding guidelines for microbial control in water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia (led by National Institutes for Food and Drug Control); III. Optimizing testing items in the standards for purified water and water for injection in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia (led by Beijing Institute for Drug Control).<br>After such procedures as investigation, drafting, review and publicity, breakthroughs have been made in the following three aspects of the finalized standards for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition:<br>I. International harmonization of the preparation method of water for injection. In General Chapter 0261 and the standard for water for injection, it is no longer specified that water for injection can only be prepared by distillation so that the Chinese Pharmacopoeia is harmonized with international mainstream pharmacopoeias and technical documents of the WHO and PIC/S.<br>II. Optimization of testing items for purified water and water for injection. (1) To reflect the fact that purified water and water for injection are products of continuous production, help manufacturers understand the overall significance of relevant standards in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia and avoid interpretation out of context, requirements for testing items including Annex 1 Purified Water and Annex 2 Water for Injection are added following the overall requirements stated in the text of General Chapter 0261. (2) Testing items are reasonably set based on the principle of tightening and optimizing testing items with reference to the EP, USP and JP and with the comprehensive consideration of principles and limit requirements of items such as conductivity, acidity or alkalinity, heavy metals, nitrate, nitrite, and ammonia. (3) Due to the limitations of the test for non-volatile substances and the controllability of current production conditions, the test for non-volatile substances is moved to "Notes", prompting manufacturers to decide whether to carry out this test based on risk assessment.<br>III. Fulfillment of main responsibilities for microbial control in water for pharmaceutical purposes. Given that the existing standards in the pharmacopoeia have not fully emphasized the concepts of in-process control and risk management for water for pharmaceutical purposes as products of continuous production, "9209 Guideline for Microbiological Monitoring and Control of Water for Pharmaceutical Purposes" is added to improve the control level of microbial contamination throughout the lifecycle of water for pharmaceutical purposes and fulfill the main responsibilities of manufacturers. This guideline is a complement to the Chinese Pharmacopoeia and existing technical documents for drug evaluation. Concurrently, the requirement of "microbiological monitoring" is introduced into General Chapter 0261 and the standards for purified water and water for injection.<br>3 Main framework and key content of the standards system of water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition<br>3.1 Main framework<br>The standards system for water for pharmaceutical purposes in the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, consisting of one general chapter (0261) (revised), two general testing methods (0681 (revised) and 0682 (not revised)), one guideline (9209) (newly added) and three monographs (purified water (revised), water for injection (revised) and sterile water for injection (not revised)), involves the overall requirements with regard to the definition, preparation method, production, usage, storage and transportation of water for pharmaceutical purposes and covers testing methods and limit requirements for physicochemical analysis and microbiological testing of water for pharmaceutical purposes, further highlighting the scientific validity, rigorousness, comprehensiveness and practicality of the standards. Revised standards are interpreted as follows.<br>3.2 Revision of General Chapter 0261 Water for Pharmaceutical Purposes<br>The revision of General Chapter 0261 focuses on the alignment with international advanced concepts and harmonization with GMP regulations. Non-distillation methods for the preparation of water for injection and control concepts of elemental impurities in ICH Q3D are introduced, and the main responsibilities of manufacturers and key risk control elements for water for pharmaceutical purposes are strengthened. Since water for pharmaceutical purposes is, in most cases, in an in-operation state and planktonic microorganisms in the water are not evenly distributed, typical microbiological test methods have limitations and often yield delayed results.<br>To solve the above problem, microbial limit tests are no longer listed separately in monographs of purified water and water for injection in international pharmacopoeias such as the EP, USP and JP. Instead, it is emphasized that, as water for pharmaceutical purposes is in a continuous circulating state in pipes and tanks, appropriate measures should be taken to prevent microbial contamination. In the USP and JP, general requirements are specially proposed regarding the classification, preparation, quality control and testing of water for pharmaceutical purposes, including microbiological monitoring requirements (including testing methods, warning limits, action limits, selection of sampling points and sampling strategies). The EP does not include general requirements for water for pharmaceutical purposes but provides detailed descriptions of microbiological monitoring as a part of production control in the monograph of purified water or water for injection.<br>The Chinese Pharmacopoeia separately lists microbial limit tests in monographs of purified water and water for injection in editions prior to the 2025 edition, and General Chapter 0261 includes very few requirements concerning the microbiological control, failing to embody the characteristics of dynamic continuous production and showing deficiencies in the monitoring, control and risk management of microbial contamination. In view of this, the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition significantly intensifies the requirements regarding microbiological monitoring and control of water for pharmaceutical purposes: (1) Microbiological monitoring is introduced into monographs of purified water and water for injection (i.e., Annex 1 and Annex 2 of General Chapter 0261). (2) The text of General Chapter 0261 lists test methods and limit criteria for microbial limit and emphasizes the requirements for quality risk management, including appropriate adjustment of the test quantity based on monitoring data, setting of warning limits and action limits, development of monitoring strategies and permission of the use of better methods. (3) 9209 Guideline for Microbiological Monitoring and Control of Water for Pharmaceutical Purposes is added, including characteristics of microorganisms in water for pharmaceutical purposes, microbiological monitoring, microbiological control and risk warning, to help pharmaceutical manufacturers enhance the concepts of process control and risk management and guide manufacturers to scientifically use rapid microbial detection techniques to well implement microbial contamination control throughout the lifecycle of water for pharmaceutical purposes.<br>3.5 Revision of quality requirements of purified water<br>In editions of the Chinese Pharmacopoeia prior to the 2025 edition, quality requirements of purified water are incorporated in the form of a monograph in Volume II, with a note "See the same monograph in Volume II" in Volume IV. In the Chinese Pharmacopoeia 2025 Edition, testing items for purified water are systematically revised based on long-standing industry demands and the actual situation of production. The testing items are incorporated in the monograph of purified water in Volume IV (no longer incorporated in Volume II).
